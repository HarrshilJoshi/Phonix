What is a PWA and Why Build One?
PWA stands for Progressive Web App. It lets your normal website behave like a real mobile app — users can install it on their phone's home screen, it works offline, and it can even be published to the Google Play Store.

Why this matters for your music app:
No need to build a separate Android app in Java/Kotlin
Users can install it directly from Chrome — no app store needed initially
Works offline using cached files (great for music apps)
Lock screen controls for music playback (play/pause/next from notification bar)
Can be published to Play Store using PWABuilder — 100% free tool

Step 2 — Create Your App Icons
Android needs icons in many different sizes. Different screen densities (hdpi, xhdpi, xxhdpi) use different sizes. Instead of creating each one manually, use a free tool.

How to generate icons (takes 2 minutes):
Go to: realfavicongenerator.net in any browser
Click 'Select your Favicon image' and upload a 512×512 PNG of your logo
Scroll down and click 'Generate your Favicons and HTML code'
Download the package — it contains all sizes
Create an /icons folder in your project
Copy all the PNG files into your /icons folder
Rename them to match: icon-72x72.png, icon-96x96.png, etc.

"orientation: portrait" — Locks app to portrait mode
"purpose: any maskable" — Allows Android to apply rounded corner mask to icon

Step 9 — Test Your PWA
Before uploading to Play Store, verify everything works correctly using Chrome DevTools:

Desktop Testing (Chrome):
Open your website in Chrome on laptop/PC
Press F12 to open DevTools
Click the 'Application' tab
Click 'Manifest' in the left sidebar — should show all your icons with no errors
Click 'Service Workers' — should show 'Status: activated and running'
Click 'Lighthouse' tab → Run audit → Should score 90+ on PWA section

Mobile Testing (Android Chrome):
Open your live HTTPS site in Chrome on Android
Tap the 3-dot menu (top right)
You should see 'Add to Home Screen' or 'Install App'
Tap it and install — the app should open without browser address bar
Turn off WiFi and open the installed app — it should still load (offline mode)


Step 10 — Upload to Google Play Store
Once your PWA is live and tested, you can publish it to the Play Store using PWABuilder — a free Microsoft tool that converts your PWA into an Android APK.

What you need before starting:
Your PWA live on HTTPS (Netlify/Vercel gives this free)
A Google Developer Account ($25 one-time fee at play.google.com/console)
A screenshot of your app at 1080×1920 pixels saved as /screenshots/mobile.png
Your Privacy Policy URL (e.g. yoursite.com/privacy.html)

PWABuilder Process:
Go to pwabuilder.com in your browser
Enter your website URL and click Start
It auto-checks your manifest, service worker, and HTTPS — fix any errors shown
Click 'Package for Stores'
Choose 'Google Play' — it generates an .aab file (Android App Bundle)
Download the .aab file and the signing key files (keep these safe!)

Play Console Upload:
Go to play.google.com/console and sign in
Click 'Create app' → Fill in app name and details
Go to 'Production' → 'Create new release'
Upload your .aab file
Fill in: App description, screenshots, privacy policy URL
Submit for review — takes 2-7 days for first submission
